Peace Corps Mexico

In 2012, I left my PhD program at Vanderbilt to join the Peace Corps, which sent me to Puebla, Mexico, where I worked on 3 projects related to technology, education and outreach.

Engineering outreach camp

During the summer of 2013, and in collaboration with both ELiTE Education and INAOE I organized a month-long summer engineering camp for 30 students from around Puebla, Mexico - with courses on Astrophysics, Chemistry, Mechatronics and Programming.

Computational Thinking

Computational Thinking is a way of teaching the fundamentals of computer science, without actually programming.

A new subject even in the US, I worked with 3 professors at my partner instutition, INAOE, to "bring Computational Thinking to Mexico." This involved developing a spanish-language curriculum, writing a textbook on the subject, and developing teacher-training workshops.

This project was the first major attempt to introduce the topic into schools in Latin America, and it culminated in Pensamiento Computacional a free online website with all the materials we developed
